A cat is a small, furry, domesticated mammal that is known for being independent and purring. Cats are part of the Felidae family, which also includes lions, tigers, and leopards. 
Physical characteristics: Cats have sharp claws and teeth, They have long tails and powerful bodies, Cats have acute senses, and Cats have specialized teeth for hunting. 
Domestication:
Cats were domesticated in the Near East around 7500 BC
People have kept cats as pets, used them as hunters, and even worshiped them as gods 
Reproduction: 
Female cats reach sexual maturity at around four months old
Gestation lasts about 64 days, and the average litter size is four kittens
Kittens are usually weaned at two months old and reach adult size by the time they're 10 months to a year old
Breeds:
There are more than 60 major cat breeds, including the Siamese, British Shorthair, and Maine Coon
